Condor Business Class Reviews: 2026 Passenger Verdict

If you've been going back and forth on whether to splurge on a premium seat for your next transatlantic flight, you're probably already deep in research mode. Condor Business Class Reviews have been picking up a lot of attention from American travelers lately, and for good reason. Most Recent Reviews from the Past 12 Months

Condor Business Class Reviews from the past year paint a pretty consistent picture. Travelers flying in 2024 and into 2026 generally describe the experience as a strong value play compared to what the big legacy carriers charge for similar transatlantic routes. On platforms like TripAdvisor, Skytrax, and airline review forums, the business class cabin earns solid marks for comfort and crew attitude.

One theme that comes up repeatedly is how pleasant the experience feels for the price point. American passengers flying from cities like New York JFK or Seattle frequently note that they got a lot more than expected given what they paid. The direct comparison that shows up most often is against carriers like Lufthansa or American Airlines on the same routes, where business class tickets can run significantly higher.

That said, not every review is glowing. Some travelers mention inconsistent Wi-Fi performance and occasional delays in meal service. But complaints of that nature are pretty common across most airlines, and they don't seem to drag the overall sentiment down by much.

What Changed in Business Class in 2026

Condor made some real updates to its premium cabin heading into 2026, and passengers are noticing. The most talked-about change is the updated seat configuration on several aircraft in the fleet. The airline expanded its lie-flat seat availability on more transatlantic routes, which has been a major selling point for long-haul travelers.

Beyond the seats, Condor also refreshed its bedding and amenity kits. Flyers have mentioned the new pillow and blanket setup as a genuine improvement over what was offered in prior years. The amenity kit, while not luxury-level, includes the basics done well: a sleep mask, earplugs, and skincare essentials that hold up fine for a seven to nine hour flight.

The entertainment system also got a refresh on newer aircraft. Screens are larger and the content library has grown, with more current movie titles and TV series available. Older planes in the fleet haven't all been updated yet, which is worth keeping in mind depending on your specific route and aircraft type.

New Food and Beverage Menu Reactions

The food is one of the most talked-about parts of any Condor Business Class Reviews discussion, and 2026 brought a revamped menu. Condor partnered with new catering suppliers for select routes, and the reaction from passengers has been mostly positive.

Breakfast and dinner service on overnight routes have received the most attention. Travelers describe the meals as restaurant-quality compared to what economy passengers receive, with multi-course setups that include a starter, main, and dessert. Portion sizes are generous, and the presentation has been called out as a step above what you'd typically expect from a leisure airline.

The beverage selection has also expanded. Business class passengers now get access to a wider wine list, including some European labels that pair well with the food. Non-alcoholic options like sparkling water, juices, and specialty coffees are available throughout the flight, not just during meal service.

A few reviewers have noted that menu options can vary significantly by route and even by departure date, so what one person experienced might not match exactly what you get. That inconsistency is the main gripe in this category, but it's not a dealbreaker for most.

Seat Product Ratings from Recent Flyers

Seat comfort is where Condor Business Class Reviews get really interesting in 2026. On routes operated by newer aircraft, the lie-flat seats are getting strong ratings. Passengers consistently mention being able to sleep through a good portion of the flight, which is the whole point of paying for business on a long transatlantic crossing.

The seat width is comparable to what other European carriers offer in business, and the storage space next to the seat is handy for keeping essentials like your phone, water bottle, and headphones within arm's reach. Privacy between seats is decent, though not as enclosed as what you'd find on a Qatar Airways or Singapore Airlines product.

On older aircraft, the seats recline to a nearly flat position rather than fully flat, and some travelers find that setup less comfortable for sleeping. If rest is your priority, it's worth checking which aircraft type is assigned to your specific flight before booking.

Crew Service Consistency in 2026 Reviews

This is one area where Condor consistently earns praise across Condor Business Class Reviews from 2026. The cabin crew in the premium cabin is frequently described as warm, attentive, and genuinely helpful. Multiple reviewers call out specific moments where crew members went out of their way to make the flight more comfortable, which is the kind of detail that sticks with people.

American passengers in particular seem to appreciate that the crew communicates well in English and doesn't make you feel like an afterthought. Service timing is generally on point, with meals arriving without long waits and drink refills coming without having to ask multiple times.

Where some inconsistency shows up is in overnight flights where the crew is managing rest for both themselves and passengers. A handful of reviews mention slower response times in the middle of the night, but that's a common complaint on pretty much any airline doing a long overnight haul.

Overall Score from Aggregated 2026 Passenger Data

Looking at aggregated ratings across multiple review platforms, Condor's business class sits in the range of 4 out of 5 stars for most categories in 2026. Seat comfort and crew service score highest. Food and beverage come in close behind. Entertainment and Wi-Fi reliability score slightly lower but are still rated as acceptable by most passengers.

For context, a Condor Airlines review breakdown on Skytrax places the airline in a solid three to four star range overall, which aligns with what individual passengers are reporting on their own.

Is Condor Business Worth Booking Right Now?

Based on what travelers are saying across the board, yes. Condor Business Class Reviews in 2026 land in a genuinely positive place for American travelers who prioritize value without completely giving up comfort. If you're comparing dollar for dollar against the major legacy carriers, Condor business class frequently comes in at a meaningfully lower price while still delivering lie-flat seats, solid food, and attentive service on most routes.

It's not going to beat the absolute top-tier carriers in the world on every metric. But for a transatlantic trip where you want to arrive rested and well-fed without spending twice as much, Condor business class is a smart, well-supported choice right now.

FAQs

Do Condor Business Class Reviews in 2026 show improvement over previous years?

Yes. Recent passenger feedback reflects noticeable improvements in seat comfort, food quality, and the overall cabin experience compared to reviews from 2022 and 2023.

What airports in the U.S. offer Condor business class service?

Condor operates transatlantic business class flights from several U.S. cities including New York JFK, Seattle, and Anchorage, with routes heading primarily to Frankfurt and other European destinations.

Is Condor business class lie-flat on all routes?

Not on every route. Lie-flat seats are available on newer aircraft, but some older planes in the fleet offer angled-flat or deep recline seats instead. Checking the aircraft type before booking is recommended.
