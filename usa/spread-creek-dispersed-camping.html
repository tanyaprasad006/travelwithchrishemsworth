Bear Safety for Dispersed Camping Jackson Wyoming Adventures

Bear safety at dispersed camping jackson wyoming locations is non-negotiable because the entire Jackson Hole region is within the Greater Yellowstone Ecosystem with significant grizzly and black bear populations throughout the Bridger-Teton National Forest. Unlike national park campgrounds that have bear boxes and active ranger monitoring, dispersed camping jackson wyoming sites have no infrastructure and require you to provide all of your own bear safety measures. All food, cookware, toiletries, trash, and any scented items must be stored in a hard-sided vehicle with windows fully closed or in a properly executed bear hang at least 12 feet off the ground and 6 feet from the trunk, or in an approved bear canister. Carry bear spray in an accessible hip holster at all times when away from your vehicle. Set up your cooking area at least 200 feet downwind from your sleeping area. Store cooking clothes with food items rather than sleeping in them. Check the Wyoming Game and Fish Department and Bridger-Teton National Forest websites for current bear activity reports before any dispersed camping jackson wyoming trip to areas with recent bear activity.
